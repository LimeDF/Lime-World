HERCULES BEETLE
Hercules beetle is the largest and one
of the most powerful beetles on Earth.
Hercules beetle can reach almost 21 cm
in length. The beetle has a strong carapace
and a powerful horn that serves
as a defense.
The color of the beetle can vary from black
to yellow-olive. The body of the beetle is
covered with small red hairs. A large horn is present only in the male. Eat a bug fruit.
The larvae of the Hercules beetle are also
huge, can reach up to 150 grams. It feeds
on roots and rotting wood. The larva has
been underground for more than a year.
Interesting facts about the Hercules beetle:
(1) The color of the beetle females is matte, while the males are glossy.
(2) A beetle can lift weights 850 times its own.
(3) Beetle larvae process rotting wood into useful soil, thereby bringing great benefit.

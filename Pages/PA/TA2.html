HUMMINGBIRD
Hummingbird is the smallest bird on earth.
Swine-like endemic bird.
In addition to nectar, hummingbirds feed on
small insects. During a dive, it can spill 400
times more than its own body per second.
Hummingbird has a long, sensitive tongue
with which she eats nectar, and touching it,
they pollinate it.
Hummingbird is small from 6 to 10 cm and
weighs only 1.7 grams. It has colorful,
multi-colored feathers that change color
depending on the lighting. And it has short
wings and a long beak, which can reach more
than the length of the body of the bird
itself. 9 tail feathers in the tail.
Hummingbird chicks are very small and
helpless creatures. Before they become
adults, they live in gengs with a diameter
of 2 to 3 cm. The beak and feathers of the
chicks takes on the usual shape and sizes
3 nidels after hatching. And after a month
the chicks leave the nest.
Interesting Hummingbird Facts:
(1) Hummingbirds can flap their wings at up to 50 times per second.
(2) Hummingbird is the only bird in the world that can fly back to front.
(3) Hummingbird heart beat 500 to 1200 times per minute.
